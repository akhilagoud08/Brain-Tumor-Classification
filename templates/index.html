What is a Brain Tumor?

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain. Tumors may be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). As the tumor grows, it can put pressure on surrounding brain tissues and affect normal brain functions.

Common symptoms include headaches, seizures, vision problems, memory loss, difficulty speaking, and loss of balance. The severity of symptoms depends on the size and location of the tumor.
